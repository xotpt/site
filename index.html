BIO

Taryn Tilton was born in Maine, raised in Illinois, and educated in Vermont. She lived in Shanghai before moving to Buenos Aires, where she has resided for the past six years.

She writes fiction and translates. She also teaches creative writing workshops at Walrus Books. Her novella Cherry Cherry won the Plaza Literary Prize and was published in 2017.

BOOKS

Cherry Cherry is about a young girl, Sophie, and her obsession with her best friend’s older sister in the summer before high school.

Sophie is more reverent towards her peers than actual adults, pained by possibility and the fleeting, aching beauty of being an adolescent girl. It's a summer full of wonder and impossible longing as Sophie decides who she is and what she wants.
